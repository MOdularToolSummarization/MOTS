[0] Princess Alexandra Caroline Marie Charlotte Louise Julia or "Alix" (as her immediate family knew her) was born at the Yellow Palace, an 18th-century town house at 18 Amaliegade, right next to the Amalienborg Palace complex in Copenhagen. [1] A few months later, Alexandra travelled from Denmark to Britain aboard the royal yacht Victoria and Albert II and arrived in Gravesend, Kent, on 7 March 1863. [2] The couple were married on 10 March 1863 at St George's Chapel, Windsor Castle, by Thomas Longley, the Archbishop of Canterbury. [3] In public, Alexandra was dignified and charming; in private, affectionate and jolly. [4] An increasing degree of deafness, caused by hereditary otosclerosis, led to Alexandra's social isolation; she spent more time at home with her children and pets. [5] Alexandra spent the spring of 1877 in Greece recuperating from a period of ill health and visiting her brother King George of the Hellenes. [6] Alexandra undertook many public duties; in the words of Queen Victoria, "to spare me the strain and fatigue of functions. [7] In 1910, Alexandra became the first queen consort to visit the British House of Commons during a debate. [8] After Alexandra married the Prince of Wales in 1863, a new park and "Palace of the People", a huge public exhibition and arts centre under construction on a hilltop overlooking north London, were renamed the Alexandra Palace and park to commemorate her. [9] The Queen Alexandra Memorial by Alfred Gilbert was unveiled on Alexandra Rose Day 8 June 1932 at Marlborough Gate, London.
